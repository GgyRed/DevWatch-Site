For many young Nigerians completing the National Youth Service Corps (NYSC), the transition into the labour market can feel overwhelming. The anxiety is understandable: the economy is tight, competition is fierce, and opportunities are unevenly distributed across the country. Yet, despite these challenges, Nigeria’s job market is not barren. It is simply fragmented—and knowing *where* to look, *what* to pursue, and *how* to position yourself can significantly shorten the journey to meaningful employment.

Recent data from the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) shows that while headline unemployment figures have declined under revised measurement methods, underemployment and informal work remain widespread. In simple terms, many Nigerians are working, but fewer are in stable, well-paid, formal roles. This reality makes strategic decision-making crucial for young professionals.
