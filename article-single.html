Rebuilding Trust in Public Institutions

Why accountability reforms continue to stall — and what could realistically change that trajectory.

By DevWatch Editorial · October 2025 · 9 min read

Public trust in institutions remains a defining challenge for governance systems across Africa. While reform agendas frequently emphasise transparency and accountability, outcomes often fall short of expectations.

Understanding the Trust Deficit

The persistence of institutional distrust is less a question of capacity and more one of incentives. Reform efforts often collide with entrenched political and bureaucratic interests.

Policy failure in this context is rarely technical; it is fundamentally political.

Why Reform Efforts Stall

Accountability mechanisms are frequently undermined by informal power structures that operate beyond formal rules. This creates a disconnect between reform design and implementation.

What Could Change

Sustainable reform requires aligning incentives, strengthening informal accountability, and building coalitions that benefit from institutional performance rather than dysfunction.
